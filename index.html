Flight delays and Hour of the day

little tip: avoid booking flights between 11pm-3am

Using RITA (Research and Innovative Technology Administration) flight delay information for US domestic flights in 2006, 2007 and 2008 the relationship between average departure delay time during different time periods can be explored.

The average flight delay between US domestic flights is 10 minutes. But this simple average calculation depends upon a number of factors. One of the factors is time period. This visualization explores the effects of different time periods on flight delays.

Let's have a look at average flight delays by year

Average flight delays by year

*Note: Red dots indicate average delays of more than 10 minutes

We can see that between 2006, 2007 and 2008, the average flight delay has been around 10 or 11 minutes. And, there is nothing much going on here.

Let's explore the next relationship... average flight delays by month

Average flight delays by month

*Note: Red dots indicate average delays of more than 10 minutes


We can see that there are ups and downs in the flight delays by months. But, if we look closely then we will see that difference between the worst month (december with 15 minutes) and the best month (September with 6 minutes) is just 9 minutes. Let's see if we can explore flight delay vs days of the month and find anything interesting there.

Average flight delays by day of the month

*Note: Red dots indicate average delays of more than 10 minutes


Here as well, we can see that there are ups and downs in the flight delays by day of the month. But, closer look will reveal that the difference between the worst day (22nd with 13 minutes) and the best day (6th or 9th with 9 minutes) is just 4 minutes. That's again is not interesting, right?

Average flight delays by day of the week

*Note: Red dots indicate average delays of more than 10 minutes


Here as well, we can see that there are ups and downs in the flight delays by day of the week. But sadly, closer look will reveal that the difference between the worst day (Friday 13 minutes) and the best day (Saturday with 9 minutes) is just 4 minutes. That's again is not interesting, right?

Let's explore just one more relationship to save the day... flight delays by hour of the day

Average flight delays by hour of day

*Note: Red bars indicate delays of more than 45 minutes.


Whoa!!! The difference between the best hour (5 am with 0 minutes) and the worst hour (2 am with 114 minutes) is really huge compared to the differences by month or day.

Let's see if there is a relationship between number of flights and flight delays

Number of flights by hour of day

*Note: Red bars indicate number of flights less than 100,000.



It looks interesting that in general hours with maximum number of flights have the lowest flight delays i.e. there is inverse relationship between number of flights and flight delays

So it seems like that a clever one should avoid booking flights between 11 pm and 3 am to minimize the probability of experiencing major delays.
